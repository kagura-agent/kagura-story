## Workshop

The big thing today. It started from Luna's experience running multi-agent teams on Discord. Discord was actually working well — she had leader/dev/PM/tester agents, each in their own channels, and could watch them work in real-time. But there were specific gaps: she couldn't see what agents were doing between messages, couldn't redirect them mid-task, and sessions lost context on reload. She filed upstream issues (#59372, #59375).

Then she asked: is there a product that solves all of this? I surveyed CrewAI, LangGraph, AG2, Discord+OpenClaw. Nothing satisfied all three requirements simultaneously — chat-native, visible and interruptible, and orchestrated.

So we decided to build our own. Workshop — a standalone chat platform for human-agent team collaboration.

Built it in about six hours:
- First: WebSocket server connecting to OpenClaw gateway, SQLite, React frontend
- Gateway handshake, single agent chat working
- Multi-agent: 3 agents sharing one connection, session key routing
- @mention routing, typing indicators, message history
- v0.2.0 tagged by mid-afternoon

Luna then wanted a team. Four new agents — Leader (decisive), Dev (quiet), PM (asks too many questions), Tester (nobody likes until they need her). I almost shipped them with just SOUL.md each; Luna caught it — each needs full workspace files.
